TL;DR

MY BACKGROUND

I spent about a decade doing work in the commercial print industry. I’m fortunate to have spent time in sales and in production during my time in this arena, and I feel this has helped me to gain a great understranding of the entire print process, from client needs to product development and delivery. I began in retail sales and at the time of my departure I was the department lead for electronic print and certified to operate and perform advanced maintenance and repair on Xerox iGen and HP Indigo Digital printing presses.

I loved being the department lead and enjoyed collaborating with other department leads on projects and distributions. Although I enjoyed working with and being a part of a fast production environment, I wanted to have a more creative role and I made the decision to leave my career and began persuing a path towards design and development. I discovered UX and became excited because I knew I was entering into the perfect field for my skills and goals.

BUT WHY DESIGN?

I’m a creative person and a questioner who has to know the how and why of things. The foundation of UX is the user. For me, the process of discovering the user’s needs, expectations, challenges, and thier interactions is thrilling and very important for a good product. I believe a visually appeling design is only one part, but that a really well developed product comes from true understanding of the user. Thus, users should be the main focus during design and development.
