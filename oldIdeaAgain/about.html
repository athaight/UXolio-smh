TL;DR

MY BACKGROUND

I spent a decade doing work in the commercial print industry. I worked in sales at first but was more drawn to production and was able to change roles. I feel having exposure to both departments helped me to gain a great understanding of the entire print process, from client needs to product development and delivery (I was naturally UX focused without knowing it). At the time of my departure I was the Lead for Electronic Print Department and had obtained certifications to operate and perform advanced maintenance and repair on Xerox iGen and HP Indigo Digital printing presses. It was a pretty big deal.

I loved being the Department Lead and enjoyed collaborating with other Department Leads on projects and distributions. Although I enjoyed working with and being a part of a fast production environment I always wanted to have a more creative role and I eventually made the decision to leave my career so I could begin pursuing a path towards design and development. When I discovered UX I was so excited because I knew I'd be entering into the perfect field for my skills and goals.

BUT WHY DESIGN?

I’m a creative person and someone who questions a lot, having to know the how and why of things. The foundation of UX is the user and the entire process the user experiences. For me, the process of discovering the user’s needs, expectations, challenges, and their interactions are the most thrilling parts, and that is very important for a good product. I believe a visually appealing design is only one part, but that a really well developed product comes from true understanding of the user. Thus, users should be the main focus during design and development.
